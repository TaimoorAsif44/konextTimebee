FAQs

How accurate is GPS tracking of employees for clock-ins and clock-outs?

TimeBee delivers high location accuracy, typically within 5 to 10 meters under normal conditions. It uses real-time GPS data to confirm that clock-ins and clock-outs happen at the right job site, helping reduce time theft and boost compliance. With built-in features like geofencing and map-based logs, our GPS time tracker for employees gives you reliable, location-linked attendance records you can trust.

What happens if you track employee location and they try to clock in outside the job site?

With TimeBee, any clock-in attempt outside the approved job site gets flagged instantly. Real-time alerts notify managers, so nothing slips through. Turn on geofencing, and it ensures employees can only clock in when they are at the right location. This location tracker app for employees is a simple way to prevent off-site check-ins, encourage accountability, and keep attendance data accurate, without slowing anyone down.

Does GPS tracking continue when the employee is off the clock?

No, GPS tracking of employees only happens during active work hours. Once an employee clocks out, location tracking stops immediately. TimeBee is designed to respect employee privacy while ensuring accountability during work hours. Employers can only view location data tied to scheduled shifts or clocked-in periods, maintaining a balance between oversight and personal privacy.

How do I handle employees working in areas with a poor GPS signal?

If your team is working in a low-signal area, the location-based time tracking still works by storing data locally on the device. Once the signal improves or internet connectivity restores, all clock-in and location data syncs automatically. By using such tools, you can prevent gaps in attendance records and maintain accuracy, even in challenging environments.

What type of device is required to use the employee time clock with GPS?

All you need is a smartphone or tablet that has built-in GPS and internet access. The app works on Android, iOS, and desktop devices, so your team can clock in from wherever they are. It is lightweight, easy to install, and runs smoothly without draining the battery.

What happens if an employee forgets to enable GPS?

If the GPS is off, the system won’t be able to track employee location accurately during clock-in. However, there are many apps that are smart enough to catch it, prompting the employee to turn on location services before they can proceed. If they skip it, the attempt may be flagged for manager review. That way, location data stays reliable, and you are always in the loop when something is missing.

Will GPS time stamps be visible to both the employee and the manager?

Yes, with the TimeBee GPS time tracking app, both employees and managers can see GPS time stamps for every clock-in and clock-out. Employees can double-check their own records, while managers get real-time location data for oversight. This ensures transparency and provides accurate, location-verified logs for both sides.

FAQs

Is it possible to track productive hours separately from non-productive hours?

Yes, when keeping track of hours worked, TimeBee lets you log productive time separately from non-productive time. It analyzes website and app usage during work sessions to identify which tools are work-related. Productive hours are calculated from approved platforms, while time spent on unrelated or unproductive sites is logged as non-productive. These classifications are defined by your organization, making it easier to assess employee focus and get a clear breakdown of efficiency in timesheets.

Can I view total hours worked by department or project?

Absolutely. With TimeBee, you can track total hours by department or project effortlessly. From the team dashboard, simply use the data filters to select a specific department and view the cumulative hours worked. You can also enable project-based tracking, where employees log work hours directly to client or internal projects. Each project collects individual contributions to give you a complete and organized timesheet summary.

How can I monitor billable vs non-billable hours for client projects?

You can use advanced software to track employee hours and get clear visibility into billable and non-billable hours for every client project. Employees log billable time directly under client-assigned tasks, while non-billable activities, like internal work, are tracked separately under organizational projects. With seamless switching between the two, teams can maintain precise records without confusion. All billable entries are automatically consolidated in the client dashboard, giving you a clear, transparent view for billing and reporting.

Can I export time data for audits or client invoicing from a work hour tracker?

Yes, you can easily export work hour data for audits or client invoicing. Reports can be downloaded in both CSV and PDF formats. You have the flexibility to export entire timesheets or filter by specific clients, departments, or employee groups. You can also apply date filters, either custom ranges or predefined periods, to customize the export to your audit or billing needs.

Is there a way to track hours per task, not just total hours?

TimeBee hourly work tracker goes beyond basic clock-in/clock-out tracking. You can create specific tasks within each project, and employees can log their hours directly under those tasks. This lets you see both the total time spent on a project and a detailed breakdown of hours by individual activity.

Can I track employee hours across different time zones?

Definitely. With TimeBee, you can easily track work hours of your team across time zones. Each hourly employee can be assigned a specific time zone, ensuring all logged entries reflect local working hours accurately. This keeps records consistent and eliminates confusion, so managers can review timesheets with confidence, no matter where the team is located.

Should I use a free employee hours tracker?

Using a free time tracker for work hours may feel like a reliable option, but it often comes with serious limitations. Most free tools lack the advanced features needed to effectively monitor hourly employees. Moreover, security protocols are typically weak, putting sensitive data at risk, and data backup options are either limited or nonexistent. If your business depends on accurate hours tracking and secure records, TimeBee is a smarter investment.

Is it better to track hours daily or weekly for performance review purposes?

An employee hours tracker works best for performance reviews when hours are tracked daily rather than weekly. A daily log gives managers a clearer view of how time is spent, helps identify issues early, and ensures ongoing accountability. It also makes it easier to measure individual contributions and provide accurate, data-backed feedback. In contrast, weekly tracking can miss small but important productivity gaps that add up over time.

What happens if someone forgets to log their work hours?

Forgetting to track working hours is not a major issue if your team is using TimeBee. If someone forgets to log their hours, TimeBee has safeguards in place to minimize any gaps. The desktop app autostarts with system boot-up and sends timely reminders to prompt employees to start tracking. These pop-ups help prevent missed entries. If time is still unlogged, the employee can manually submit a request for that period by entering the project, task details, a description, and the approver's name. Thanks to these built-in measures, all work remains accurately recorded, even if logging happens later.

FAQs

How to keep track of contractor hours?

Tracking contractor hours is essential for controlling costs while keeping construction projects on schedule. TimeBee, a popular time tracking app for contractors in the construction industry, automates this process with precision. It offers real-time visibility into daily labor activity through automatic hour logs. Whether supervising crews across multiple job sites or monitoring labor costs, TimeBee enables managers to maintain control without needing to be on-site.

How can time tracking software for contractors help prevent buddy punching on construction sites?

Construction time tracking software helps prevent buddy punching by capturing GPS location data at every clock-in or clock-out. Therefore, only workers physically present at the job site can log hours, which ensures greater accuracy. By linking each entry to a verified location, these applications promote accountability while reducing time fraud across construction teams.

Can workers clock in and out even if they are in areas with limited or no internet access?

Yes, construction workers can clock in and out without internet access because of TimeBee’s advanced offline tracking feature. The app saves time entries and location data locally on the worker’s device. Once connectivity is restored, all information syncs automatically to the central system, ensuring no loss of data.

How can construction time tracking assist in dispute resolution regarding hours worked?

TimeBee’s contractor time tracking software in construction plays a key role in resolving disputes by providing clear, timestamped records for every clock-in and clock-out. Not only that, but since each labor entry links to specific dates, times, and job site locations, it creates a dependable audit trail. This transparency simplifies verification of claims while speeding up dispute resolution.

Can foremen or supervisors approve manual time entries before submitting them to payroll?

Yes, TimeBee allows foremen or supervisors to review manual time entries before payroll hours submission. If a crew member misses clocking in or out, they can submit a manual time request with details such as the time range, approver’s name, task, and a brief description. The assigned approver receives the request and decides to accept or reject it based on the information provided. This approval process ensures accountability while offering flexibility for genuine time entry corrections.

Can construction companies use time tracking data for project costing and budgeting?

Yes, construction companies can use contractor time tracking data to improve project costing and budgeting. By logging billable hours across different construction projects or tasks, TimeBee delivers detailed insights into where labor time is spent. This allows managers to allocate labor costs accurately plus compare estimated versus actual hours. Over time, the recorded data helps construction businesses refine budgets, forecast future expenses, and ensure profitability.

What is the best way to introduce digital time tracking to workers who are used to paper timesheets?

The best approach is to start with a time tracking app for contractors that offers a minimal learning curve and easy usability. Also, select a solution that closely mimics the simplicity of paper logging while delivering clear benefits like automatic hour calculation and real-time updates. Once you have made your pick, conduct a brief hands-on training session to guide crews through basic steps like clocking in and selecting tasks. Emphasize how digital tracking minimizes manual errors while simplifying their work process.

Does the contractor time tracking software show the GPS location of each time entry for the construction crew?

Yes, TimeBee's time clock app records GPS location data for every time entry made by the construction crew. Each time a team member clocks in or clocks out via the mobile app, their time is captured alongside their GPS coordinates. This information is compiled into detailed timesheets, giving managers full visibility of where and when each crew member worked.

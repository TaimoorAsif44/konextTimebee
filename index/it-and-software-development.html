FAQs

How can I see which projects or tasks are consuming the most developer hours?

You can use TimeBee software to track time spent on projects and see which ones consume the most software development hours. Its web dashboard highlights projects’ progress, ranked from the highest to the lowest logged hours. Within each project, tasks are also organized by time spent, making it easy to spot priorities. You can even go further to see which team members contributed the most hours to each task, all in a clear, structured view.

Can I view individual and team-level time breakdowns?

Yes, TimeBee makes it easy to view both individual and team-level time breakdowns through dedicated dashboards. It displays key time categories, including productive, unproductive, neutral, idle, manual, and unrated time, for entire software teams as well as individual developers and groups. This comprehensive visibility allows companies to monitor teams effectively, identify inefficiencies, reduce non-billable hours, and make informed decisions to optimize performance across their development workflows.

Can I get automated reports for tracking developer productivity, payroll, or invoices?

With TimeBee, you can automatically receive reports on developer productivity, payroll, and invoices. Productivity is measured through devs’ web and application usage, which companies can customize as productive, unproductive, neutral, or unrated. The billable/non-billable tracker helps calculate payroll by using tracked hours and pay rates for accurate payments. You can add extra compensation to calculated salaries using adjustment options. For clients, invoices are auto-generated based on billable hours and pay rates set in their individual dashboards.

What options do I have for customizing access levels and settings?

TimeBee offers flexible control over access and settings through three user roles, i.e., member, admin, and owner. While members can only view their own logs, admins and owners have access to all team data. Beyond access levels, settings can be customized at both the user and organization levels, too. Organization settings cover general preferences like currency and time zone, while user settings include options such as screenshot frequency, shift timings, hourly rate, minimum tracked hours, and more.

What measures are in place to ensure data security and confidentiality?

With sensitive data involved, security becomes a top concern when using time-tracking software for programmers. TimeBee addresses this with robust measures, including an end-to-end encrypted dashboard that ensures only invited users can access organizational data based on their roles. Additionally, TimeBee complies with industry-standard security protocols and data protection regulations, giving companies the confidence to monitor performance without compromising confidentiality or exposing critical information.

How can I ensure accurate client billing based on developer time logs?

Accurate client billing is ensured by allowing developers to log their hours directly within the client’s dedicated dashboard. Each time entry is tied to specific projects and tasks, giving full visibility into the work done. Organizations also set custom salary rates for each developer assigned to a client. Therefore, TimeBee automatically calculates bills using the logged billable hours and assigned rates.

How does time tracking software for IT professionals differentiate between productive and unproductive time?

TimeBee’s developer time tracking tool distinguishes between productive and unproductive time by letting organizations rate websites and applications based on their own criteria. Time spent is measured against these ratings to assess productivity at individual, team, or organization levels. For added flexibility, platforms can also be marked as neutral or left unrated to avoid misclassification.

Can I see idle time per user or across the entire team in a weekly report?

Yes, TimeBee provides clear insights into idle time for individual users or entire teams through both the web dashboard and email reports. You can filter reports by user, group, or time range to view the daily, weekly, or monthly idle time as needed. Since idle time reflects inactivity, it is treated as non-billable and automatically excluded from total tracked hours for accurate billing and payroll.

FAQs

What legal factors should you consider before using an employee monitoring app?

Using employee monitoring software legally starts with obtaining clear, informed consent from your employees, ideally through signed agreements. Monitoring should be limited to company-owned devices whenever possible to reduce privacy concerns. If employees are using personal laptops, they must have full control over when monitoring begins or ends. Transparency is critical: clearly communicate what data is being collected, how it will be stored, and who will have access to it. Finally, make sure your monitoring practices comply with relevant data protection laws like the GDPR, and follow local labor regulations to stay on the right side of the law.

What activities can I monitor on employee devices during work hours?

During work hours, you can monitor employee computer activity to ensure productivity and focus. These include web and app usage to distinguish between work-related tools and distractions, monitoring logged hours to verify attendance and active time, and reviewing task or project progress to assess both individual and team performance. Periodic screenshots can also provide visual context to validate ongoing work without interrupting workflows.

Can I use employee performance monitoring software to see which websites or apps employees use during the day?

Yes, these apps let you track which websites and applications your team uses throughout the workday. The software logs all activity and uploads it to a centralized dashboard, where each site or app is initially marked as unrated. Admins can then categorize them as productive, unproductive, or neutral. Once categorized, the dashboard displays a breakdown of the most-used websites and apps across your organization, including detailed time spent by each employee, from highest to lowest.

Is it possible to track idle time vs. active work time in an employee tracking app?

TimeBee can accurately differentiate between idle time and active work time. Idle time is logged when no mouse or keyboard activity is detected, while active time reflects consistent user interaction. To reduce prolonged inactivity, TimeBee sends a distraction alert after a preset period of idle time. A pop-up appears on the employee’s screen, prompting them to re-engage. Once they respond, active monitoring and time logging automatically resume.

Can the employee tracker take screenshots of what employees are working on?

Definitely, these apps can automatically capture screenshots of screen activity at set intervals. Admins can configure the screenshot frequency for each employee based on the required level of oversight. Available options include every 2, 3, 5, 10, 15, 20, or 30 minutes, with a “never” option to disable screenshots entirely when not needed. All captured images are securely stored in the web dashboard, each with an exact timestamp for easy reference.

Will employees know that I am using employee tracker software to monitor them?

Yes, employees are fully aware when monitoring is active with TimeBee. Ethical tracking starts with clear, informed consent, and TimeBee does not support silent or hidden monitoring. Employees have complete control over when to start and stop the timer for each tracked session. Additionally, they can access their own logged data through the web dashboard, allowing them to review their activity and identify performance gaps on their own.

How does monitoring software for employees track screen activity across multiple monitors or tabs?

TimeBee tracks screen activity by focusing only on the active tab or window being used, not every open tab or background program. Therefore, time calculations are based solely on the tab or application currently being engaged with, providing accurate insight into where attention is directed. This approach eliminates inflated usage logs and offers a true reflection of work activity.

How do I measure productivity based on activity monitored?

Using software to monitor employees, you can measure productivity by analyzing how much of an employee’s active time is spent on websites and applications labeled as productive. The system calculates a productivity percentage by dividing productive time by total active time. This provides a clear, data-driven snapshot of individual performance and work habits.

If I monitor employee computer, will it slow down their devices or interfere with work?

No, monitoring employee computers with a lightweight solution like TimeBee won’t slow down devices or disrupt daily tasks. The software runs silently in the background, using minimal system resources to track activity without affecting performance. Employees can continue working across multiple applications with no interruptions to their workflow.

How does staff monitoring software store employee data, and is it secure?

Hybrid and remote employee monitoring software like TimeBee ensures data security through an encrypted dashboard that prevents unauthorized access. Role-based permissions ensure that only Admin users can view all employee data, while Member-level users can access only their own activity logs. TimeBee also complies with key data protection laws, including GDPR, CCPA, and ECPA, ensuring that employee information is collected, stored, and used lawfully.

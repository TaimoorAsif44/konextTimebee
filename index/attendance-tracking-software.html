FAQs

Can the time and attendance app handle both remote and on-site employee attendance?

Yes, TimeBee supports both remote and on-site teams by offering flexible, web-based tracking. Employees can log their time in employee attendance software from any location, while managers get accurate records, including clock-in, clock-out, total tracked hours, etc. Attendance settings can be customized for each user or across the entire organization, allowing control over working days, shift times, and required hours. Not only that, but the late arrival and early leave margins help ensure precise, rule-based attendance tracking.

Can I use the app to track employee attendance and integrate the data with payroll processing?

Yes. Advanced monitoring tools like TimeBee allow seamless integration between attendance tracking and payroll processing. Instead of just logging work hours, the app automatically syncs each employee’s recorded time with its built-in payroll module. These hours are matched with the employee’s hourly rate to calculate accurate pay. Once processed, payroll reports can be exported, shared with finance teams, or uploaded to accounting software.

Can employees clock in on the attendance monitoring software from mobile devices?

Yes. With TimeBee, employees can clock in from both mobile devices and desktops. The platform offers full functionality on both web and mobile, allowing users to log hours from whichever device fits their work environment. Whether they are remote, on-site, or on the go, employees can record attendance in real time. Unlike desktop-only solutions, TimeBee supports flexibility, making attendance tracking more reliable and efficient.

How does the attendance tracker software flag or report late arrivals and early departures?

In a time attendance app, late arrivals and early departures are flagged based on predefined attendance rules. Organizations can set these rules at the individual or company level by defining shift start and end times, along with acceptable margins for lateness and early departures. Once these settings are configured, the app automatically compares each employee’s time logs against the schedule. If an employee clocks in after the late margin, they are marked as “late” on the attendance sheet. Similarly, clocking out before the early leave margin triggers an “early leave” status, ensuring accurate, rule-based reporting across the board.

How do I handle flexible hours with the employee attendance app?

Flexible hours can be easily managed by adjusting the attendance settings within the app. To allow flexibility, you can set the working days to “any” and disable fixed shift start and end times. This removes rigid scheduling while still enabling accurate tracking. To maintain accountability, you can also define minimum required hours per day, so employees have the freedom to choose when they work, as long as they meet expected daily durations.

Can the employee attendance software confirm a user’s presence at a client location?

Yes, Timebee uses GPS-based logging to verify if the employee was actually present at a client location. Its mobile version allows employees to clock in and out with a single tap, while also capturing their GPS location at each entry. This location data is stored alongside the time logs, giving managers clear visibility into whether the employee was physically at the correct client site when marking attendance.

How long is attendance data stored in the staff attendance software?

In most attendance software, data is stored and accessible for up to one year. During this period, administrators can view, filter, and export historical records by date, user, or team. Keeping a full year of detailed logs helps organizations make informed decisions, maintain accurate records, and stay compliant with internal policies and external workforce regulations.

Is tracking employee attendance across multiple locations or branches possible?

You need a centralized time attendance app like Timebee that supports multi-location management. First, set up each branch or office as a separate team within the system. You can then assign employees to their respective teams and configure time zones alongside attendance settings based on each branch’s needs.

Should I use a free attendance tracker to monitor my employees’ clock-in and clock-out times?

While free attendance trackers can log basic clock-in and clock-out times, they lack essential features like GPS verification, customizable shift settings, reliable data storage, and more. These limitations can lead to inaccurate records, limited scalability, and poor accountability. Timebee, on the other hand, offers a more robust and dependable solution with advanced features designed for accurate and secure attendance tracking.

FAQs

How can I monitor billable vs. non-billable time for the entire agency?

You can use a time tracking software for agencies to monitor billable vs. non-billable hours. Set up a centralized system where every team member logs their hours against specific tasks or projects. Make sure each time entry is categorized as either billable or non-billable. With built-in reporting tools, you can then analyze this data to gain full visibility into how time is allocated. This method helps you optimize resource management, boost project profitability, and ensure client invoices are accurate and transparent.

Can I track billable time for different clients and projects separately?

Yes, when time tracking for agencies, you can easily log billable hours separately for each client and project. For example, with the TimeBee app, you can create distinct client profiles and individual projects, then assign specific tasks to team members. As hours are logged against these tasks, the system automatically tracks time by client and project. This setup allows you to generate detailed reports, simplify payroll processing, and gain valuable insights to improve profitability and balance workloads effectively.

What kind of reports can I generate to monitor the team performance in my agency?

Using the TimeBee app, you can create detailed reports that offer clear insights into your team’s performance and project status. These include tracking overall activity, quarterly progress, attendance, and project milestones, helping you manage productivity and meet deadlines. Additionally, profitability and project costing reports give you a thorough view of your finances, allowing you to control budgets and keep projects profitable.

Can I review and approve timesheets before they are submitted for billing?

Yes, many time tracking software for marketing agencies include a feature that lets managers or team leads review and approve timesheets before they are finalized for billing. This process ensures logged hours are accurate, align with project requirements, and allows for necessary adjustments. By using timesheet approval, you gain better control over billing accuracy and enhance overall project management.

Can I restrict access to time data by role or permission level?

Yes, time tracking software like TimeBee lets you control access to time data through a structured role and permission system. Agency owners have full access to all organizational data, settings, billing, and user management. Admins can manage projects, review team reports, approve time entries, and monitor performance. Meanwhile, members can log their own time, view assigned tasks, and access only their personal reports. This setup ensures sensitive information stays secure and is visible only to the appropriate users.

Can I track time for both retainer clients and one-off projects?

Yes, most creative agency time tracking software allows you to track time for both retainer clients and one-off projects. You can set up separate project structures for ongoing retainers, where you can log time against recurring tasks, as well as for one-time projects with defined scopes and deadlines. With such flexibility, you can manage different client relationships effectively and ensure proper billing for ongoing and short-term engagements.

Is it possible to track time spent on internal agency development work vs. client work?

You can use time tracking software for marketing agencies to monitor time spent on internal agency development work and client work separately. Most apps allow you to create distinct project categories for non-billable efforts like team training, process improvements, or internal marketing, while tracking billable hours for client projects. This helps you see where your team's time goes, allocate resources wisely, and assess how internal work contributes to your agency’s overall performance.

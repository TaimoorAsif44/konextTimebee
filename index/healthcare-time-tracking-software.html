FAQs

Can the healthcare time tracking app track time for both clinical and administrative staff across multiple departments?

Yes, a time tracking app for the healthcare industry allows you to monitor work hours for both clinical and administrative staff across multiple departments. Whether it's nurses, doctors, or front desk personnel, the app ensures accurate time logging, shift scheduling, and department-based reporting. With TimeBee, you can streamline health care time tracking and workforce management by offering centralized visibility through its dashboard and real-time updates. Within the app, you can assign roles, set department-specific rules, and analyze team productivity for every employee regardless of their job title.

Can healthcare employees clock in and out using mobile devices?

A powerful healthcare time and attendance software lets employees easily clock in and out using their mobile devices. This is especially helpful for on-the-go medical staff, such as home healthcare providers, visiting nurses, and emergency medical staff. Apart from attendance monitoring, the mobile clock-in features include GPS tracking and time stamps for better accountability and location accuracy. So once the staff is clocked in, marks their attendance, and has to leave the hospital premises for an emergency or other hospital work, their time is logged in.

How can I differentiate between billable and non-billable hours for medical practitioners?

You can use a time clock for healthcare services to classify hours as billable or non-billable with ease. During time entry or shift scheduling, hospital and clinic administration or owners using the app can tag tasks or patient visits as billable, such as consultations or home visits. At the same time, administrative duties or meetings can be marked non-billable depending on the employee's job description requirements. Top apps like TimeBee can automatically calculate totals and generate separate reports for both categories. This saves you the time and effort of calculating the accurate payable amount based on precise time data.

Can I generate timesheets and reports for payroll and audit purposes?

Absolutely, with a hospital time tracker, you can generate detailed timesheets and reports for both payroll and audit purposes. Most apps have built-in payroll calculators based on the tracked time or required efficiency levels. However, if you want to calculate separately, use the hospital time clock that captures every clock-in, clock-out, break, overtime entry, and attendance. This gives you every detail you need to calculate accurate payroll and audits. These reports can be customized by date range, department, or role, and integrated with payroll software to automate calculations and reduce errors.

Can healthcare professionals track time offline in remote or low-connectivity areas?

Yes, many solutions for healthcare time tracking offer offline functionality. Staff can clock in and out, record visits, and log notes without an internet connection. These apps store the data locally and automatically sync it once connectivity is restored. The offline feature of home health care time tracking software ensures that no productive time is missed. This is especially beneficial for caregivers working in low-connectivity areas. If someone's offline minutes are not recorded, they can request manual time addition for accuracy.

Is there role-based access control to limit who can view or edit specific details?

Timesheet software for healthcare typically includes role-based access control (RBAC) to protect sensitive data. In most hospitals and clinics, access is granted based on hierarchy. Department heads or supervisors may be given administrative access, while general staff can only view their own records. If the hospital has a dedicated HR or admin department, only those teams can approve timesheets or make edits. This layered access helps maintain security, supports HIPAA compliance, and minimizes the risk of administrative errors.

FAQs

Can I approve or reject manual entries before timesheets are submitted for payroll?

Yes, you can review manual entries and either approve or reject them before they are added to payroll. Using TimeBee, employees can submit time requests for inactive slots by filling in details such as project, task, description, and approver. These requests are then reviewed by the approver, who can accept or reject them based on accuracy. This process ensures that only verified hours make it to payroll and keeps manual adjustments under control.

Should I use a free timesheet app or invest in a paid one?

Free timesheet solutions might look attractive at first, but they often lack key features, dependable support, and strong data protection. As your team expands or project needs grow, these gaps can cause errors and slowdowns. A paid application like TimeBee, on the other hand, offers accurate tracking and advanced tools designed to meet real business demands.

Is it possible to set up different hourly rates for different team members?

Yes, you can assign different hourly rates to each team member. TimeBee lets you set internal rates for payroll and separate external rates for client billing. Whether the rates differ based on skills, experience, or roles, the system tracks all hours and calculates pay using each member’s assigned rate.

Can I restrict access so managers only see timesheets for their own team?

TimeBee allows you to control who can see what. It offers three role types: members, admins, and the owner. Members can only view their own timesheets, which promotes personal accountability without exposing other users' data. Contrarily, admins and the owner have full access to all team timesheets for complete oversight and management.

Is there an option to track idle time or inactivity on a timesheet tracking app?

Absolutely, TimeBee accurately tracks idle time and inactivity. Idle time is recorded when there is no mouse or keyboard movement for a set time. Then, a prompt appears asking the user to resume work. If there is no response within a minute, that time is marked as inactive. Inactive time is excluded from total tracked hours, helping ensure timesheets stay accurate.

How does the app handle overtime calculations and reporting?

TimeBee tracks overtime by recording hours worked beyond scheduled shifts. For flexible roles, any time over the minimum daily requirement is marked as overtime. These extra hours are automatically labeled in attendance timesheets for easy reference. Overtime is also calculated in payroll using a separate predefined rate, ensuring employees are compensated accurately.

Can I track time across multiple projects for a single employee?

Yes, you can use project-based tracking to log time across multiple projects for any team member. Employees can log hours under both internal and external projects, ensuring each entry is tied to the correct task. Even when switching between tasks or handling multiple clients, all tracked time stays clearly organized by project.

What features should a reliable timesheet software for small business have?

A good timesheet tool for small businesses should include accurate time tracking, project-based logs, idle time detection, and manual entry approvals. These features help maintain accountability without requiring constant oversight. Automated reports should also display attendance, performance, and payroll hours clearly. Moreover, the tool should also offer real-time updates, exportable reports, and mobile compatibility to support teams across different locations.
